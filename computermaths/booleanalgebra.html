Boolean Algebra and Digital Circuits
Boolean algebra is the branch of algebra in which the values of the variables are the truth values true and false, usually denoted 1 and 0 respectively. Instead of elementary algebra where the values of the variables are numbers, and the main operations are addition and multiplication, the main operations of Boolean algebra are the conjunction and∧, the disjunction or, ∨, and the negation not, denoted ¬.
