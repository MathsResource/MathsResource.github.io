Graph Theory

Graph theory is the study of points and lines. In particular, it involves the ways in which sets of points, called vertices, can be connected by lines, called edges. Graphs are classified according to their complexity, the number of edges allowed between any two vertices, and whether or not directions are assigned to edges.
